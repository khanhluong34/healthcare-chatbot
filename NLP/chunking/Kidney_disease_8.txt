Kidney disease Kidney disease Treatment approaches for kidney disease focus on managing the symptoms, controlling the progression, and also treating co-morbidities that a person may have. Millions of people across the world have kidney disease.  Of those millions, several thousand will need dialysis or a kidney transplant at its end-stage.  In the United States, as of 2008, 16,500 people needed a kidney transplant.  Of those, 5,000 died while waiting for a transplant.  Currently, there is a shortage of donors, and in 2007 there were only 64,606 kidney transplants in the world.
