Arthritis Arthritis  Arthritis is more common in women than men at all ages and affects all races, ethnic groups and cultures.  In the United States a CDC survey based on data from 2013 to 2015 showed 54. 4 million  adults had self-reported doctor-diagnosed arthritis, and 23. 7 million  had arthritis-attributable activity limitation .  With an aging population, this number is expected to increase.  Adults with co-morbid conditions, such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity, were seen to have a higher than average prevalence of doctor-diagnosed arthritis .
