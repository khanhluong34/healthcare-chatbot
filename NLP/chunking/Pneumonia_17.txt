Pneumonia Pneumonia In general, in adults, investigations are not needed in mild cases.  There is a very low risk of pneumonia if all vital signs and auscultation are normal.  C-reactive protein  may help support the diagnosis.  For those with CRP less than 20 mg/L without convincing evidence of pneumonia, antibiotics are not recommended. Procalcitonin may help determine the cause and support decisions about who should receive antibiotics.
