Individuals with arthritis can benefit from both physical and occupational therapy.  In arthritis the joints become stiff and the range of movement can be limited.  Physical therapy has been shown to significantly improve function, decrease pain, and delay the need for surgical intervention in advanced cases.  Exercise prescribed by a physical therapist has been shown to be more effective than medications in treating osteoarthritis of the knee.
