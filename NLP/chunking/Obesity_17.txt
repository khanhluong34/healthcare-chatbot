Obesity Obesity  World trends in active leisure time physical activity are less clear.  The World Health Organization indicates people worldwide are taking up less active recreational pursuits, while research from Finland found an increase and research from the United States found leisure-time physical activity has not changed significantly.  Physical activity in children may not be a significant contributor. In both children and adults, there is an association between television viewing time and the risk of obesity.  Increased media exposure increases the rate of childhood obesity, with rates increasing proportionally to time spent watching television. Like many other medical conditions, obesity is the result of an interplay between genetic and environmental factors.
