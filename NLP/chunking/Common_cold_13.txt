Common cold Common cold  In 2009, Canada restricted the use of over-the-counter cough and cold medication in children six years and under due to concerns regarding risks and unproven benefits.  The misuse of dextromethorphan  has led to its ban in a number of countries.  Intranasal corticosteroids have not been found to be useful. In adults short term use of nasal decongestants may have a small benefit.  Antihistamines may improve symptoms in the first day or two; however, there is no longer-term benefit and they have adverse effects such as drowsiness.  Other decongestants such as pseudoephedrine appear effective in adults.
