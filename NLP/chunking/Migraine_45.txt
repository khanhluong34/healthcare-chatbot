Migraine Migraine  In the United States, direct costs have been estimated at $17 billion, while indirect costs – such as missed or decreased ability to work – is estimated at $15 billion.  Nearly a tenth of the direct cost is due to the cost of triptans.  In those who do attend work during a migraine attack, effectiveness is decreased by around a third.  Negative impacts also frequently occur for a person's family. Transcranial magnetic stimulation shows promise, as does transcutaneous supraorbital nerve stimulation.  There is preliminary evidence that a ketogenic diet may help prevent episodic and long-term migraine. While no definitive proof has been found linking migraine to gender, statistical data indicates that women may be more prone to having migraine, showing migraine incidence three times higher among women than men Migraine Migraine
