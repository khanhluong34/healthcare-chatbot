Hepatitis C Hepatitis C  It is a member of the genus Hepacivirus in the family Flaviviridae.  There are seven major genotypes of HCV, which are known as genotypes one to seven.  The genotypes are divided into several subtypes with the number of subtypes depending on the genotype.  In the United States, about 70% of cases are caused by genotype 1, 20% by genotype 2 and about 1% by each of the other genotypes.  Genotype 1 is also the most common in South America and Europe. The half life of the virus particles in the serum is around 3 hours and may be as short as 45 minutes.  In an infected person, about 1012 virus particles are produced each day Hepatitis C Hepatitis C
