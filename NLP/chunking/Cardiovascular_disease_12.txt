Studies have shown that short sleepers getting less than seven hours sleep per night have a 10 percent to 30 percent higher risk of cardiovascular disease. Sleep disorders such as sleep-disordered breathing and insomnia, are also associated with a higher cardiometabolic risk. 
An estimated 50 to 70 million Americans have insomnia, sleep apnea or other  chronic sleep disorders. In addition, sleep research displays differences in race and class.  Short sleep and poor sleep tend to be more frequently reported in ethnic minorities than in whites.
