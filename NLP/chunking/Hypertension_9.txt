Hypertension Hypertension  Insulin resistance, which is common in obesity and is a component of syndrome X , also contributes to hypertension. Events in early life, such as low birth weight, maternal smoking, and lack of breastfeeding may be risk factors for adult essential hypertension, although the mechanisms linking these exposures to adult hypertension remain unclear.  An increased rate of high blood uric acid has been found in untreated people with hypertension in comparison with people with normal blood pressure, although it is uncertain whether the former plays a causal role or is subsidiary to poor kidney function.  Average blood pressure may be higher in the winter than in the summer.  Periodontal disease is also associated with high blood pressure. Secondary hypertension results from an identifiable cause.
