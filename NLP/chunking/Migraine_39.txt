Migraine Migraine  Migraine does not appear to increase the risk of death from stroke or heart disease.  Preventative therapy of migraines in those with migraine with aura may prevent associated strokes.  People with migraine, particularly women, may develop higher than average numbers of white matter brain lesions of unclear significance. Worldwide, migraine affects nearly 15% or approximately one billion people.  It is more common in women at 19% than men at 11%.  In the United States, about 6% of men and 18% of women experience a migraine attack in a given year, with a lifetime risk of about 18% and 43% respectively.
