Infectious diseases Infectious diseases The top three single agent/disease killers are HIV/AIDS, TB and malaria.  While the number of deaths due to nearly every disease have decreased, deaths due to HIV/AIDS have increased fourfold.  Childhood diseases include pertussis, poliomyelitis, diphtheria, measles and tetanus.  Children also make up a large percentage of lower respiratory and diarrheal deaths.  In 2012, approximately 3. 1 million people have died due to lower respiratory infections, making it the number 4 leading cause of death in the world.
