Type 1 diabetes can occur at any age, and a significant proportion is diagnosed during adulthood.  Latent autoimmune diabetes of adults  is the diagnostic term applied when type 1 diabetes develops in adults; it has a slower onset than the same condition in children.  Given this difference, some use the unofficial term "type 1. 5 diabetes" for this condition.  Adults with LADA are frequently initially misdiagnosed as having type 2 diabetes, based on age rather than a cause.
