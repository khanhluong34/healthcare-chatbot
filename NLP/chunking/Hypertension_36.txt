Hypertension Hypertension  Several other agents were developed after the Second World War, the most popular and reasonably effective of which were tetramethylammonium chloride, hexamethonium, hydralazine, and reserpine .  None of these were well tolerated.  A major breakthrough was achieved with the discovery of the first well-tolerated orally available agents.  The first was chlorothiazide, the first thiazide diuretic and developed from the antibiotic sulfanilamide, which became available in 1958.  Subsequently, beta blockers, calcium channel blockers, angiotensin converting enzyme  inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers, and renin inhibitors were developed as antihypertensive agents. The World Health Organization has identified hypertension, or high blood pressure, as the leading cause of cardiovascular mortality.
