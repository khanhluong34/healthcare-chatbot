Common cold Common cold The common cold is generally mild and self-limiting with most symptoms generally improving in a week.  In children, half of cases go away in 10 days and 90% in 15 days.  Severe complications, if they occur, are usually in the very old, the very young, or those who are immunosuppressed.  Secondary bacterial infections may occur resulting in sinusitis, pharyngitis, or an ear infection.  It is estimated that sinusitis occurs in 8% and ear infection in 30% of cases. The common cold is the most common human disease and affects people all over the globe.  Adults typically have two to three infections annually, and children may have six to ten colds a year  Common cold Common cold
