Cancer Cancer  In females, the most common types are breast cancer, colorectal cancer, lung cancer, and cervical cancer.  If skin cancer other than melanoma were included in total new cancer cases each year, it would account for around 40% of cases.  In children, acute lymphoblastic leukemia and brain tumors are most common, except in Africa, where non-Hodgkin lymphoma occurs more often.  In 2012, about 165,000 children under 15 years of age were diagnosed with cancer.  The risk of cancer increases significantly with age, and many cancers occur more commonly in developed countries.
