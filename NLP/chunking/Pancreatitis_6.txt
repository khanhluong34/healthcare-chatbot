Pancreatitis Pancreatitis  Blood loss, dehydration, and fluid leaking into the abdominal cavity  can lead to kidney failure.  Respiratory complications are often severe.  Pleural effusion is usually present.  Shallow breathing from pain can lead to lung collapse.  Pancreatic enzymes may attack the lungs, causing inflammation.  Severe inflammation can lead to intra-abdominal hypertension and abdominal compartment syndrome, further impairing renal and respiratory function and potentially requiring management with an open abdomen to relieve the pressure.
