Hepatitis Hepatitis  Certain factors may portend a poorer outcome, such as co-morbid medical conditions or initial presenting symptoms of ascites, edema, or encephalopathy.  Overall, the mortality rate for acute hepatitis is low: ~0. 1% in total for cases of hepatitis A and B, but rates can be higher in certain populations . In contrast to hepatitis A & B, hepatitis C carries a much higher risk of progressing to chronic hepatitis, approaching 85–90%.   Cirrhosis has been reported to develop in 20–50% of patients with chronic hepatitis C. Other rare complications of acute hepatitis include pancreatitis, aplastic anemia, peripheral neuropathy, and myocarditis.
