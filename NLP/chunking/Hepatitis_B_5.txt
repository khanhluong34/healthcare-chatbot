Hepatitis B Hepatitis B  The disease is most prevalent in Africa  and in the Western Pacific region .  Infection rates are 1. 5% in Europe and 0. 5% in the Americas.  According to some estimates, about a third of the world's population has been infected with hepatitis B at one point in their lives.  Hepatitis B was originally known as "serum hepatitis". Acute infection with hepatitis B virus is associated with acute viral hepatitis, an illness that begins with general ill-health, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, body aches, mild fever, and dark urine, and then progresses to development of jaundice Hepatitis B Hepatitis B
