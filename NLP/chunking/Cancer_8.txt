Cancer Cancer  Ulceration can cause bleeding that can lead to symptoms such as coughing up blood , anemia or rectal bleeding , blood in the urine , or abnormal vaginal bleeding .  Although localized pain may occur in advanced cancer, the initial tumor is usually painless.  Some cancers can cause a buildup of fluid within the chest or abdomen. Systemic symptoms may occur due to the body's response to the cancer.  This may include fatigue, unintentional weight loss, or skin changes.  Some cancers can cause a systemic inflammatory state that leads to ongoing muscle loss and weakness, known as cachexia.
