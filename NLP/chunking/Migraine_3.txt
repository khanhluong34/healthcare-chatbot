Migraine Migraine Migraine typically presents with self-limited, recurrent severe headache associated with autonomic symptoms.  About 15–30% of people living with migraine experience episodes with aura, and they also frequently experience episodes without aura.  The severity of the pain, duration of the headache, and frequency of attacks are variable.  A migraine attack lasting longer than 72 hours is termed status migrainosus.
