While the throat always contains bacteria, potentially infectious ones reside there only at certain times and under certain conditions.  A minority of types of bacteria such as Mycobacterium tuberculosis and Legionella pneumophila reach the lungs via contaminated airborne droplets.  Bacteria can also spread via the blood.  Once in the lungs, bacteria may invade the spaces between cells and between alveoli, where the macrophages and neutrophils  attempt to inactivate the bacteria.  The neutrophils also release cytokines, causing a general activation of the immune system.  This leads to the fever, chills, and fatigue common in bacterial pneumonia Pneumonia Pneumonia
