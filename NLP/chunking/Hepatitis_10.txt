Hepatitis Hepatitis S.  Alcoholic hepatitis is within the spectrum of alcoholic liver disease.  This ranges in order of severity and reversibility from alcoholic steatosis , alcoholic hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer .  Hepatitis usually develops over years-long exposure to alcohol, occurring in 10 to 20% of alcoholics.  The most important risk factors for the development of alcoholic hepatitis are quantity and duration of alcohol intake.  Long-term alcohol intake in excess of 80 grams of alcohol a day in men and 40 grams a day in women is associated with development of alcoholic hepatitis .  Alcoholic hepatitis can vary from asymptomatic hepatomegaly  to symptoms of acute or chronic hepatitis to liver failure Hepatitis Hepatitis
