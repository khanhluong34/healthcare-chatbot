During this time period, insurance companies realized the connection between weight and life expectancy and increased premiums for the obese. Many cultures throughout history have viewed obesity as the result of a character flaw.  The obesus or fat character in Ancient Greek comedy was a glutton and figure of mockery.  During Christian times the food was viewed as a gateway to the sins of sloth and lust.  In modern Western culture, excess weight is often regarded as unattractive, and obesity is commonly associated with various negative stereotypes.
