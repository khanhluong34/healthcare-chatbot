Urethritis Urethritis  If left untreated, the bacteria that cause non-gonococcal urethritis can lead to various complications.  In males, complications can lead to epididymitis, reactive arthritis, conjunctivitis, skin lesions, and discharge.  In females, complications can lead to pelvic inflammatory disease, chronic pelvic pain, vaginitis, mucopurulent cervicitis, and miscarriages. The disease is classified as either gonococcal urethritis, caused by Neisseria gonorrhoeae, or non-gonococcal urethritis , most commonly caused by Chlamydia trachomatis, which is accounted for 20-50% of routinely tested cases.  NGU, sometimes called nonspecific urethritis , has both infectious and noninfectious causes.
