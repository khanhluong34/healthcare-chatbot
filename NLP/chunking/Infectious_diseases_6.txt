Disease can arise if the host's protective immune mechanisms are compromised and the organism inflicts damage on the host.  Microorganisms can cause tissue damage by releasing a variety of toxins or destructive enzymes.  For example, Clostridium tetani releases a toxin that paralyzes muscles, and staphylococcus releases toxins that produce shock and sepsis.  Not all infectious agents cause disease in all hosts.
