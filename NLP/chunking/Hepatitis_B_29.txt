Hepatitis B Hepatitis B  Polyarteritis nodosa is more common in people with hepatitis B infection. A number of different tests are available to determine the degree of cirrhosis present.  Transient elastography  is the test of choice, but it is expensive.  Aspartate aminotransferase to platelet ratio index may be used when cost is an issue. Hepatitis B virus DNA remains in the body after infection, and in some people, including those that do not have detectable HBsAg, the disease recurs.
