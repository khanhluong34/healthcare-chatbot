In the developing world urbanization is playing a role in increasing rate of obesity.  In China overall rates of obesity are below 5%; however, in some cities rates of obesity are greater than 20%.  In part, this may be because of urban design issues .  Time spent in motor vehicles, as opposed to active transportation options such as cycling or walking, is correlated with increased risk of obesity. Malnutrition in early life is believed to play a role in the rising rates of obesity in the developing world.
