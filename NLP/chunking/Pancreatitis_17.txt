Globally the incidence of acute pancreatitis is 5 to 35 cases per 100,000 people.  The incidence of chronic pancreatitis is 4–8 per 100,000 with a prevalence of 26–42 cases per 100,000.  In 2013 pancreatitis resulted in 123,000 deaths up from 83,000 deaths in 1990. In adults in the United Kingdom, the estimated average total direct and indirect costs of chronic pancreatitis is roughly £79,000 per person on an annual basis.
