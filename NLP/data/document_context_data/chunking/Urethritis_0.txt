Urethritis Urethritis Urethritis is the inflammation of the urethra.  The most common symptoms include painful or difficult urination and urethral discharge.  It is a commonly treatable condition usually caused by infection with bacteria.  This bacterial infection is often sexually transmitted, but not in every instance; it can be idiopathic, for example.  Some incidence of urethritis can appear asymptomatic as well. Symptoms vary based on the cause of the diseases.
