Bipolar disorder  involves abnormally "high" or pressured mood states, known as mania or hypomania, alternating with normal or depressed moods.  The extent to which unipolar and bipolar mood phenomena represent distinct categories of disorder, or mix and merge along a dimension or spectrum of mood, is subject to some scientific debate.
