Common cold Common cold A cold usually begins with fatigue, a feeling of being chilled, sneezing, and a headache, followed in a couple of days by a runny nose and cough.  Symptoms may begin within sixteen hours of exposure and typically peak two to four days after onset.  They usually resolve in seven to ten days, but some can last for up to three weeks.  The average duration of cough is eighteen days and in some cases people develop a post-viral cough which can linger after the infection is gone.
