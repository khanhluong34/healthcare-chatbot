Hypertension Hypertension Hypertension , also known as high blood pressure , is a long-term medical condition in which the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated.  High blood pressure usually does not cause symptoms.  High blood pressure, however, is a major risk factor for stroke, coronary artery disease, heart failure, atrial fibrillation, peripheral arterial disease, vision loss, chronic kidney disease, and dementia.  Hypertension is a major cause of premature death worldwide. High blood pressure is classified as primary  hypertension or secondary hypertension.
