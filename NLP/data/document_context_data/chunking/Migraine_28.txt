Migraine surgery, which involves decompression of certain nerves around the head and neck, may be an option in certain people who do not improve with medications. There are three main aspects of treatment: trigger avoidance, acute symptomatic control, and medication for prevention.  Medications are more effective if used earlier in an attack.  The frequent use of medications may result in medication overuse headache, in which the headaches become more severe and more frequent.  This may occur with triptans, ergotamines, and analgesics, especially opioid analgesics.
