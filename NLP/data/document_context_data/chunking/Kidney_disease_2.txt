Diabetic nephropathy is a progressive kidney disease caused by angiopathy of the capillaries in the glomeruli.  It is characterized by nephrotic syndrome and diffuse scarring of the glomeruli.  It is particularly associated with poorly managed diabetes mellitus and is a primary reason for dialysis in many developed countries.
