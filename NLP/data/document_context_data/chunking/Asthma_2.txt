Asthma Asthma  Treatment of rapidly worsening symptoms is usually with an inhaled short-acting beta-2 agonist such as salbutamol and corticosteroids taken by mouth.  In very severe cases, intravenous corticosteroids, magnesium sulfate, and hospitalization may be required. In 2019 asthma affected approximately 262 million people and caused approximately 461,000 deaths.  Most of the deaths occurred in the developing world.  Asthma often begins in childhood, and the rates have increased significantly since the 1960s.  Asthma was recognized as early as Ancient Egypt.
