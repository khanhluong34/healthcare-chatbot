Hypertension Hypertension  Nevertheless, primary or essential hypertension accounts for most cases. Hypertension is the most important preventable risk factor for premature death worldwide.  It increases the risk of ischemic heart disease, strokes, peripheral vascular disease, and other cardiovascular diseases, including heart failure, aortic aneurysms, diffuse atherosclerosis, chronic kidney disease, atrial fibrillation, cancers, leukemia and pulmonary embolism.  Hypertension is also a risk factor for cognitive impairment and dementia.  Other complications include hypertensive retinopathy and hypertensive nephropathy.
