Meningitis Meningitis  Meningitis can be life-threatening because of the inflammation's proximity to the brain and spinal cord; therefore, the condition is classified as a medical emergency.  A lumbar puncture, in which a needle is inserted into the spinal canal to collect a sample of cerebrospinal fluid , can diagnose or exclude meningitis. Some forms of meningitis are preventable by immunization with the meningococcal, mumps, pneumococcal, and Hib vaccines.  Giving antibiotics to people with significant exposure to certain types of meningitis may also be useful.  The first treatment in acute meningitis consists of promptly giving antibiotics and sometimes antiviral drugs.  Corticosteroids can also be used to prevent complications from excessive inflammation.  Meningitis can lead to serious long-term consequences such as deafness, epilepsy, hydrocephalus, or cognitive deficits, especially if not treated quickly Meningitis Meningitis
