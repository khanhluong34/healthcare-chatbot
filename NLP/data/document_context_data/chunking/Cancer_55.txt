People with cancer have an increased risk of blood clots in their veins which can be life-threatening.  The use of blood thinners such as heparin decrease the risk of blood clots but have not been shown to increase survival in people with cancer.  People who take blood thinners also have an increased risk of bleeding. Although extremely rare, some forms of cancer, even from an advanced stage, can heal spontaneously.
