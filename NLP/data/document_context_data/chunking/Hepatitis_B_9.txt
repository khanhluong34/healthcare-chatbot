Early life horizontal transmission can occur through bites, lesions, certain sanitary habits, or other contact with secretions or saliva containing HBV.  Adult horizontal transmission is known to occur through sexual contact, blood transfusions and transfusion with other human blood products, re-use of contaminated needles and syringes.  Breastfeeding after proper immunoprophylaxis does not appear to contribute to mother-to-child-transmission  of HBV. Hepatitis B virus  is a member of the hepadnavirus family.
