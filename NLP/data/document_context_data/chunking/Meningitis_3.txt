Meningitis Meningitis  The classic triad of diagnostic signs consists of neck stiffness, sudden high fever, and altered mental status; however, all three features are present in only 44–46% of bacterial meningitis cases.  If none of the three signs are present, acute meningitis is extremely unlikely.  Other signs commonly associated with meningitis include photophobia  and phonophobia .  Small children often do not exhibit the aforementioned symptoms, and may only be irritable and look unwell.  The fontanelle  can bulge in infants aged up to 6 months.  Other features that distinguish meningitis from less severe illnesses in young children are leg pain, cold extremities, and an abnormal skin color.
