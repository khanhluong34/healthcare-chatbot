Hepatitis C Hepatitis C  Spontaneous resolution following acute infection appears more common in females and in patients who are younger, and may be influenced by certain genetic factors.  Chronic HCV infection may also resolve spontaneously months or years after the acute phase has passed, though this is unusual. The World Health Organization estimated in a 2021 report that 58 million people globally were living with chronic hepatitis C as of 2019.  About 1. 5 million people are infected per year, and about 290,000 people die yearly from hepatitis C-related diseases, mainly from liver cancer and cirrhosis. Hepatitis C infection rates increased substantially in the 20th century due to a combination of intravenous drug abuse and the reuse of poorly sterilized medical equipment.
