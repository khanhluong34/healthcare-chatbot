Hepatitis B Hepatitis B  Membranous glomerulonephritis is the most common form.  Other immune-mediated hematological disorders, such as essential mixed cryoglobulinemia and aplastic anemia have been described as part of the extrahepatic manifestations of HBV infection, but their association is not as well-defined; therefore, they probably should not be considered etiologically linked to HBV. Transmission of hepatitis B virus results from exposure to infectious blood or body fluids containing blood.  HBV is 50 to 100 times more infectious than human immunodeficiency virus .  HBV can be transmitted through several routes of infection.  In vertical transmission, HBV is passed from mother to child  during childbirth.
