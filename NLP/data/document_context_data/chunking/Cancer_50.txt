Like hospice care, palliative care attempts to help the patient cope with their immediate needs and to increase comfort.  Unlike hospice care, palliative care does not require people to stop treatment aimed at the cancer. Multiple national medical guidelines recommend early palliative care for patients whose cancer has produced distressing symptoms or who need help coping with their illness.  In patients first diagnosed with metastatic disease, palliative care may be immediately indicated.  Palliative care is indicated for patients with a prognosis of less than 12 months of life even given aggressive treatment.
