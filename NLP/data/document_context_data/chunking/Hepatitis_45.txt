Hepatitis Hepatitis  Carrier rates in developed nations are significantly lower, encompassing less than 1% of the population.  In endemic regions, transmission is thought to be associated with exposure during birth and close contact between young infants. Chronic hepatitis C is a major cause of liver cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma.  It is a common medical reason for liver transplantation due to its severe complications.  It is estimated that 130–180 million people in the world are affected by this disease representing a little more than 3% of the world population.  In the developing regions of Africa, Asia and South America, prevalence can be as high as 10% of the population.
