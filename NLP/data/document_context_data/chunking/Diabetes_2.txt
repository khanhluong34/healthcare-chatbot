Diabetes Diabetes  The global expenditure on diabetes-related healthcare is an estimated USD760 billion a year. The classic symptoms of untreated diabetes are unintended weight loss, polyuria , polydipsia , and polyphagia .  Symptoms may develop rapidly  in type 1 diabetes, while they usually develop much more slowly and may be subtle or absent in type 2 diabetes. Several other signs and symptoms can mark the onset of diabetes although they are not specific to the disease.  In addition to the known symptoms listed above, they include blurred vision, headache, fatigue, slow healing of cuts, and itchy skin.  Prolonged high blood glucose can cause glucose absorption in the lens of the eye, which leads to changes in its shape, resulting in vision changes.
