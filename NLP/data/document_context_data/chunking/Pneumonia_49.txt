Pneumonia Pneumonia  In 1887, Jaccond demonstrated pneumonia may be caused by opportunistic bacteria always present in the lung. Sir William Osler, known as "the father of modern medicine", appreciated the death and disability caused by pneumonia, describing it as the "captain of the men of death" in 1918, as it had overtaken tuberculosis as one of the leading causes of death at the time.  This phrase was originally coined by John Bunyan in reference to "consumption" .  Osler also described pneumonia as "the old man's friend" as death was often quick and painless when there were much slower and more painful ways to die. Viral pneumonia was first described by Hobart Reimann in 1938.  Reimann, Chairman of the Department of Medicine at Jefferson Medical College, had established the practice of routinely typing the pneumococcal organism in cases where pneumonia presented.
