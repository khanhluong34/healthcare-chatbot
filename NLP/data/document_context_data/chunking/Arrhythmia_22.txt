Arrhythmia Arrhythmia  AV nodal reentrant tachycardia is often curable by ablating one of the pathways in the AV node .  Atrial fibrillation can also be treated, by performing a pulmonary vein isolation, but the results are less reliable. Arrhythmias due to medications have been reported since the 1920s with the use of quinine.  In the 1960s and 1970s problems with antihistamines and antipsychotics were discovered.
