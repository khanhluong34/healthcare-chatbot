Back pain Back pain  Common underlying mechanisms include degenerative or traumatic changes to the discs and facet joints, which can then cause secondary pain in the muscles and nerves and referred pain to the bones, joints and extremities.  Diseases and inflammation of the gallbladder, pancreas, aorta and kidneys may also cause referred pain in the back.  Tumors of the vertebrae, neural tissues and adjacent structures can also manifest as back pain. Back pain is common; approximately nine of ten adults experience it at some point in their lives, and five of ten working adults experience back pain each year.  Some estimate that as many of 95% of people will experience back pain at some point in their lifetime.
