Obesity Obesity Some modifications to the WHO definitions have been made by particular organizations.  The surgical literature breaks down class II and III or only class III obesity into further categories whose exact values are still disputed. As Asian populations develop negative health consequences at a lower BMI than Caucasians, some nations have redefined obesity; Japan has defined obesity as any BMI greater than 25 kg/m2 while China uses a BMI of greater than 28 kg/m2. The preferred obesity metric in scholarly circles is the body fat percentage  – the ratio of the total weight of person's fat to his or her body weight, and BMI is viewed merely as a way to approximate BF%.  Levels in excess of 32% for women and 25% for men are generally considered to indicate obesity. BMI ignores variations between individuals in amounts of lean body mass, particularly muscle mass.
