Diagnosing meningitis as promptly as possible can improve outcomes.  There are no specific signs or symptoms that can indicate meningitis, and a lumbar puncture  to examine the cerebrospinal fluid is recommended for diagnosis.  Lumbar puncture is contraindicated if there is a mass in the brain  or the intracranial pressure  is elevated, as it may lead to brain herniation.  If someone is at risk for either a mass or raised ICP , a CT or MRI scan is recommended prior to the lumbar puncture.  This applies in 45% of all adult cases.
