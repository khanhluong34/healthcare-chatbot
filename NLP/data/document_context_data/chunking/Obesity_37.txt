In earlier historical periods obesity was rare, and achievable only by a small elite, although already recognised as a problem for health.  But as prosperity increased in the Early Modern period, it affected increasingly larger groups of the population.  Prior to the 1970s, obesity was a relatively rare condition even in the wealthiest of nations, and when it did exist it tended to occur among the wealthy.  Then, a confluence of events started to change the human condition.  The average BMI of populations in first-world countries started to increase and, consequently, there was a rapid increase in the proportion of people overweight and obese Obesity Obesity
