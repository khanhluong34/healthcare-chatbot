Hepatitis C Hepatitis C  In addition to replicating in the liver the virus can multiply in lymphocytes. Percutaneous contact with contaminated blood is responsible for most infections; however, the method of transmission is strongly dependent on both geographic region and economic status.  Indeed, the primary route of transmission in the developed world is injection drug use, while in the developing world the main methods are blood transfusions and unsafe medical procedures.  The cause of transmission remains unknown in 20% of cases; however, many of these are believed to be accounted for by injection drug use. Injection drug use  is a major risk factor for hepatitis C in many parts of the world.  Of 77 countries reviewed, 25  were found to have a prevalence of hepatitis C of between 60% and 80% among people who use injection drugs.
