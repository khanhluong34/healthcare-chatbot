Diabetes Diabetes Some cases of diabetes are caused by the body's tissue receptors not responding to insulin ; this form is very uncommon.  Genetic mutations  can lead to defects in beta cell function.  Abnormal insulin action may also have been genetically determined in some cases.  Any disease that causes extensive damage to the pancreas may lead to diabetes .  Diseases associated with excessive secretion of insulin-antagonistic hormones can cause diabetes .  Many drugs impair insulin secretion and some toxins damage pancreatic beta cells, whereas others increase insulin resistance .
