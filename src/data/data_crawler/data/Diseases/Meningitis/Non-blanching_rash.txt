A non-blanching rash  is a skin rash that does not fade when pressed with, and viewed through, a glass.It is a characteristic of both purpuric and petechial rashes. Individual purpura measure 3–10 mm ;height:1px;margin:-1px;overflow:hidden;padding:0;position:absolute;width:1px}3⁄32-3⁄8 in), whereas petechiae measure less than 3 mm.A non-blanching rash can be a symptom of bacterial meningitis, but this is not the exclusive cause.This dermatology article is a stub.
